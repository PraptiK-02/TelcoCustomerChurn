## Business Context
Customer churn poses a significant threat to subscription-based businesses by reducing recurring revenue and increasing acquisition costs. This analysis was conducted to identify the primary factors contributing to customer churn and to recommend data-driven strategies to improve retention.

## Key Findings
- Month-to-month contracts have the highest churn rate, indicating low customer commitment
- Customers with short tenure are significantly more likely to churn
- Higher monthly charges correlate with increased churn
- Customers using electronic check payment methods churn more frequently

## Recommendations
- Introduce incentives to convert customers to long-term contracts
- Strengthen early customer onboarding and engagement strategies
- Implement pricing optimization or bundled service offerings
- Encourage adoption of auto-payment methods

## Expected Impact
Applying these strategies can reduce customer churn, increase lifetime value, and improve revenue stability through improved customer retention.
